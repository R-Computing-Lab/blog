It's official! 😁 I have tenure now (as of July 1st), and I want to start writing more about the R side of my work. It's the part of my research that I enjoy the most, and I want to share it with others. Heck, I even named my lab the [R Computing Lab](https://github.com/R-Computing-Lab)... This blog is a place for me to write about the R packages I maintain, the methods I develop, and the reproducible research practices I use. 


A few things to expect here:

- **Package news** — updates to [BGmisc](https://doi.org/10.32614/CRAN.package.BGmisc), [ggpedigree](https://doi.org/10.32614/CRAN.package.ggpedigree), [discord](https://doi.org/10.32614/CRAN.package.discord), and [NlsyLinks](https://doi.org/10.32614/CRAN.package.NlsyLinks) — new features, breaking changes, and the reasoning behind them.
- **Tutorials** — worked examples using these packages: building pedigrees, running discordant kinship comparisons, and similar family-based designs, in R.
- **Research notes** — shorter posts connecting new papers or preprints back to the code and data behind them.

See the [Software](/software/) page for the full package list and the [Publications](/publications/) page for the research these tools support.
Posts are also available via [RSS](/index.xml) and on [GitHub](https://github.com/R-Computing-Lab/blog).
